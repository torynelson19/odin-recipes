Chicken Wings

Description

Chicken wings are a crowd-pleasing favorite, known for their crispy exterior, tender meat, and bold flavors. Whether served as a game-day appetizer, party snack, or casual dinner, chicken wings are endlessly versatile. They can be fried, baked, or grilled and coated in a variety of sauces, from tangy buffalo and smoky barbecue to sweet honey garlic or zesty lemon pepper. This combination of textures and flavors makes chicken wings a universally loved dish that’s both satisfying and fun to eat.

Perfect for sharing, chicken wings bring people together with their finger-licking goodness and customizable flavor options. Whether you prefer them spicy, sweet, or savory, chicken wings are easy to adapt to your taste. Pair them with classic accompaniments like celery sticks, carrot sticks, and a side of ranch or blue cheese dressing for dipping, and you’ve got a dish that’s sure to delight everyone at the table.

Ingredients

  • Chicken Wings
  • Buffalo Sauce

Steps

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). If using an air fryer, preheat it according to the manufacturer’s instructions.
  2. Prepare the Chicken Wings: Pat the chicken wings dry with paper towels to help them crisp up during cooking. Place them in a large bowl.
  3. Arrange on a Baking Sheet: Line a baking sheet with aluminum foil or parchment paper for easy cleanup. Place a wire rack on top to allow the wings to cook evenly. Arrange the wings in a single layer on the rack, ensuring they aren’t overcrowded.
  4. Bake the Wings: Bake the wings for 40–50 minutes, flipping them halfway through to ensure even cooking. If using an air fryer, cook the wings at 375°F (190°C) for 20–25 minutes, shaking the basket halfway through.
  5. Prepare the Buffalo Sauce: While the wings are cooking, warm the buffalo sauce in a small saucepan over low heat or in the microwave. Stir occasionally to ensure it's smooth and ready to coat the wings.
  6. Toss the Wings in Sauce: Once the wings are golden brown and crispy, remove them from the oven or air fryer. Transfer the wings to a large bowl and pour the buffalo sauce over them. Toss until the wings are evenly coated.
  7. Serve and Enjoy: Serve the buffalo wings hot with celery sticks, carrot sticks, and ranch or blue cheese dressing on the side for dipping.
